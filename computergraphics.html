B.C.A. (Part - III) Examination, 2026

COMPUTER GRAPHICS

Time: 3 Hours Maximum Marks: 40 / 50

Note: Attempt all questions from Section A. Attempt all questions from Section B, choosing either (a) or (b) from each.

SECTION - A (Short Answer Type)

(5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

  1. Define Aspect Ratio and Resolution.

    Hint: Relates to display dimensions and pixel density. (Unit 1)

  2. What is the difference between 4-connected and 8-connected regions in polygon filling?

    Hint: Important concept for Boundary/Flood fill. (Unit 2)

  3. What is the need for homogeneous coordinates in 2D transformations?

    Hint: Allows combining translation with scaling/rotation via matrix multiplication. (Unit 3)

  4. Define Clipping. Why is it necessary?

    Hint: Removing parts of the picture outside the viewing window. (Unit 4)

  5. What is Morphing?

    Hint: Animation technique transforming one image into another. (Unit 5)

SECTION - B (Long Answer Type)

(5 x 6 = 30 Marks)

Q. 1. (a) Explain the working of Cathode Ray Tube (CRT) with a neat and labelled diagram.

OR

(b) Differentiate between Raster scan and Random scan systems.

Q. 2. (a) Write and explain Bresenham's Line Drawing Algorithm for |m| < 1.

OR

(b) Explain Boundary Fill algorithm. How does it differ from Flood Fill algorithm?

Q. 3. (a) What is composite transformation? Prove that two successive rotations are additive: R(θ1) . R(θ2) = R(θ1 + θ2).

OR

(b) Find the transformation matrix that reflects a polygon across the line y = x.

Q. 4. (a) Explain Cohen-Sutherland line clipping algorithm with an example.

OR

(b) Explain Window to Viewport mapping and derive the transformation matrix for it.

Q. 5. (a) Explain the Z-Buffer (Depth Buffer) algorithm for visible surface detection.

OR

(b) Differentiate between Parallel and Perspective projections. Explain different types of parallel projections.
